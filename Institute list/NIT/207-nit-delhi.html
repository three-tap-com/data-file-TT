About the Institute

National Institute of Technology Delhi (NITD) is one of the thirty NIT(s) established in the year 2010 by an act of parliament and has been declared as an Institute of National importance.

NIT Delhi is an autonomous Institute which functions under the aegis of Ministry of Education, Government of India. It aims to provide instructions and research facilities in various disciplines of Engineering, Science and Technology, Management, Social Sciences and Humanities for advance learning and dissemination of knowledge. The Institute has taken great leaps and is creating waves among the Intelligentsia and world of academia.

NIT Delhi has started its academic session in 2010 with three undergraduate B.Tech degree programmes in Computer Science and Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering and Electrical and Electronics Engineering. The academic activities of NIT Delhi were initiated at NIT Warangal in year 2010 which later moved to a temporary campus at Dwarka, New Delhi in June 2012 and then at IAMR Campus, Narela (February 2014) to December 2021. The institute is running its academic programs from the permanent campus since January 2022.

Possession of fifty one acre land has been acquired for permanent campus of NIT Delhi on NH-1, Narela sub city, New Delhi.
The institute is presently function with all its academic and administrative works from the permanent campus. The phase B of the construction is commencing from August 2022. From 2022, institute has started B.Tech. in Mechanical Engineering and B.Tech. in Civil Engineering. In 2023, institute started B.Tech. program in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science. From 2024, institute is starting B.Tech. in Aerospace Engineering and B.Tech. in VLSI Design and Technology.

Location and Accessibility

National Institute of Technology Delhi is being run from its permanent campus located on the GT Karnal Road, Zone P1 of North-west Delhi district of NCT. It is well connect by rail, road. Nearest Metro station is Jahangirpuri (12 kms) on yellow line of Delhi Metro rail system, whereas the airport is located at distance of 38 kms nearly 1 hour by road.
