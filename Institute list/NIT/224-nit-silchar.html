About the Institute

National Institute Of Technology, Silchar (राष्ट्रीय प्रौद्योगिकी संस्थान, सिलचर) is one of the 31 National Institutes of Technology of India and was established in 1967 as a Regional Engineering College in Assam. In 2002 it was upgraded to the status of National Institute of Technology and was declared as Institute of National Importance under the National Institutes of Technology Act, 2007.

Student Life at Institute

The Institute recognizes that a major concern to all students and parents is the availability and quality of accommodation and food. There are separate hostels for boys and girls. There are 21 furnished hostels; 13 for boys, 6 for girls, one Married Scholar Hostel and one for Foreign students . To ensure security in Hostels (Boys/Girls), 24 hour security guards are available.

Most importantly among the  21 hostels, 2 hostels are exclusively for accommodation of B. Tech. 1st semester students (boys). All first year girl students will be accommodated in a single hostel.

Facilities in each room are furnished with cots, study tables, and storage space. Basic amenities are provided  in the form of LCD TV  in common room of each hostel. Sports facilities like Volley ball, table tennis, lawn tennis, cricket, chess board, badminton are available in the hostels. Various student related cultural & technical activities like Technoesis, Incandesence are being orgainsed in the Institute.

Location and Accessibility

NIT Silchar is located in the SIlchar. Its distance is 8 km and 10 km from the heart of the Silchar city and Silchar railway station, respectively.  Silchar is the headquarters of Cachar district in the state of Assam in India. Silchar is connected to rest of the country by rail, roads and air.

By Flight:
Flight ( Air India, SpiceJet & Jet Airways) runs everyday from Silchar to Kolkata and Guwahati and back. There are daily flighs from Kolkata and from Guwahati. The road distance of NIT Silchar is about 35 km from the Silchar airport.

By Train:
There are weekly trains from Delhi to Silchar (Sampark Kranti Express).  Kanchanjunga Express runs on three (3) days between Silchar and Kolkata. There are daily trains from Silchar to Guwahati. The road distance of NIT Silchar is about 10 km from the Silchar railway station. 

By Bus:
Buses run from state capital Guwahati (ISBT) to Silchar. There are plenty of deluxe (AC and Non-AC with 2X2 and 1X2 seating options and sleeper coach bus) buses runs in the evening from Guwahati (night super). Day super bus is also available from Guwahati. From bus station (may be ISBT, Silchar or from Silchar town) to NIT Silchar campus, fare is Rs. 150.00 to 200.00 in an auto rickshaw.

For stay at Silchar, hotels are available at Silchar town.

Plan early and avoid late moment rush as Silchar is a small town.
