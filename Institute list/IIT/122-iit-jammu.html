About the Institute 

Welcome to IIT Jammu! 

Our campus is spread over 400 acres, at the foothills of the Shivalik range of the Himalayas, at Jagti, a forest area near Jammu City at NH 44. 

The first batch of 82 students was admitted in 2016 and started functioning in a temporary campus. The operations in the Jagti campus commenced from August 2017. 

Our Jagti campus is well equipped to meet all the basic needs of the students and faculty. From an academic and research perspective, IIT Jammu has 12 academic departments fully equipped with the state-of-the-art equipment. From a student's perspective it has modern hostel buildings for girls and boys with fully equipped sports facilities. 

IIT Jammu has campus wide Wi-Fi and 1 Gbps internet connectivity. The institute has a functional e-Governance Solution (EG/SARAL) that caters to end-to-end lifecycle of students and employees covering all offices viz Academics, Student Affairs, Human Resources, Accounts, and Procurement. The studio-like 40 lecture halls provide an great ambience for learning. 

The state-of-the-art High Performance Computing (HPC) facility known as Agastya became operational in November 2020 and serves all computing needs. The Central Instrumentation Facility (CIF) known as Saptarshi is setup in Paloura campus and houses several high-end research equipment to serve all basic research needs at molecular level. 

Thus, IIT Jammu is all set to provide an all-round learning environment matching the majesty of the Himalayas, and the beauty of Jammu and Kashmir.

Philosophy 
The curriculum has been structured to be interdisciplinary and hands-on to cater to the global community's rapidly changing scientific /technological needs. Research focus and Job-market requirements in the current era have been cutting across all disciplines (eg: Bio-engineering, IoT, AI, Data Science, Smart Cities, Energy for the Future, Health Care, Functional and Interactive Devices, Advanced Materials for Innovative Applications to advance our life quality, Smart Manufacturing, Smart Cities, Water Resources, Climate Change, Himalayan Region Logistics, etc.). In such a scenario, we must make flexible and permeable boundaries between various engineering disciplines and programs offered in the Institution. At the same time, we aim to maintain the core flavours of the programs.

Engineering is all about solving real-life problems using the sound knowledge of Mathematics and Sciences. And these smart solutions are cutting across boundaries of conventional engineering disciplines and require a proper understanding of how our society functions and its cultural ethos, interpersonal skills, behavioural patterns, humanistic management, etc. 

The design of IIT Jammu academic curriculum is based on the fundamental premise that the students are coming in the programs to learn, to innovate, and solve the interdisciplinary issues that we need to address by evolving novel engineering system design. The IIT Jammu programs shall target broad expectations from Bachelor’s education in engineering to: 

(a) Impart knowledge of engineering, technology, and science fundamentals to encourage critical thinking. It will be done in consonance with humanistic and societal needs. Through the structure of our proposed programs, we would encourage the creative design of engineering systems with a connection to a National perspective.

(b) Develop sound fundamentals and technical and soft skills to enable relearning in the future.

(c) Inculcate professional and ethical conduct.

(d) IIT Jammu would like to position itself as an active science-based engineering and technology education lab. Our programs shall aim to create value through entrepreneurial effort.

(e) The courses in the programs shall be targeted to “uncover the cores, rather than covering everything”. This approach shall form the basis for innovation.

Implementation 
A four-year undergraduate bachelor's program has been designed to fulfill a particular program's course requirements and expose students to the fundamental concepts of all branches of engineering, technology, and humanities. Emphasis has been laid on devoting a substantial portion of the total credits to experimental education through laboratory experiments and hands-on projects across disciplines. Engineering and basic sciences courses are accompanied by the corresponding lab training in addition to various projects that will be taken up by the students. Design & Practicum education has been incorporated in the academic curriculum from the first semester to introduce, excite, and encourage students and to open avenues for each student to experiment and execute their ideas as they are full of energy and curiosity after their school education. Outreach projects to provide engineering and technological solutions to the real-life problems faced by society and industry are an integral part of the academic curriculum. Research components will be dispersed throughout the program. 

A concrete program has been proposed that gives equal importance to the Core Engineering subjects, Engineering Foundation, Essential Basic and Applied Science Foundation, HSS core, and Design Practicum. Introduction to various branches of engineering gives them the flexibility to do interdisciplinary projects (like transport, systems development, integrated novel solutions, green energy, health care, waste management, new energy resources, complex manufacturing, etc.), which will give them the freedom to harness their skills in any discipline.

Students are encouraged to get a minor degree in any engineering/applied sciences/humanities/management area to fulfil their inter-discipline interests. The curriculum also aims to give a choice to focus and go deeper into an area of specialization. 

By obtaining the degree, students of IIT Jammu must be able to

(i) Pursue higher studies in their domain / in any interdisciplinary area.

(ii) Adapt to emerging engineering/scientific/technological trends.

(iii) Be a sensitive and responsible engineer, innovator, job creator, and great human being.

The academic curriculum is a credit-based system. UG Rules and Regulations will describe discipline-wise credits to be earned and other modalities. Students can pursue additional Minor area degrees/Honors degrees by earning a certain number of credits. Each credit is equivalent to 1 hour of classroom teaching / 2 hours of laboratory training. It also requires 2-3 hrs of beyond-classroom study per credit. Further details can be obtained from the academic office.

Rules For Change Branch


No Change of Branch
There is no provision of branch change in IIT Jammu curriculum. It is due to the provision of interdisciplinary nature of newly devised curriculum with a much wider common core encompassing many engineering disciplines. 

Many other features like design and research component, humanities breadth with social relevance, and minor area options are available to suit the students in exploring different Engineering disciplines. The curriculum template has been structured to be inter-disciplinary and hands-on in nature to cater to the rapidly changing scientific/technological needs of the global community.

Faculty


IIT Jammu has a good mix of vibrant young and experienced senior faculty in all disciplines. We have faculty members from diverse areas of research in science and engineering. All faculty members have prior experience from different reputed International / National Institutions. IIT Jammu faculty are engaged in multi-disciplinary research with generous financial support from many Government and National / International agencies in collaboration with faculty from different National / International institutes. About 143 faculty members are currently associated with the institution, teaching regular courses. 

Detailed faculty profiles are available on Our Faculty page on the institute website. A snapshot of active research and areas of interest is available on the Areas of Research page on the website. 

IIT Jammu gets great support from faculty at IITs and Adjunct Faculty visiting the campus. 

Facilities


The on-campus facilities include hostel accommodation for all students closer to the academic area, cafeteria, open gym, indoor gym, laundry centers, digital classrooms, ubiquitous anytime Wi-Fi, music room, TT tables, snooker table, Medical Unit with 24-hour paramedic staff, student counseling, a common hall with TV, open spaces for in-campus recreational activities. SBI IIT Jammu branch is operational. HDFC, ICICI, and J&K Banks have ATMs alongside the hostels near the cafeteria. The Jagti campus is self-sufficient for most of the needs of our students. 

A 664-room Fulgar Hostel (G+7) building and Fulgar Dining Hall (G+3) building became operational in August 2023. Starting in 2024, a new Lecture Hall Complex (LHC), Mansar, with 14 classrooms, a grand Academic Block building, Pushkar, with 10 classrooms and 25 engineering labs, faculty offices, and a Mechanical and Civil workshop building, Shivsagar, have increased the overall capacity for classrooms and departmental space requirements. A new sports complex, Chinar, with multiple sports courts (Squash, Badminton, TT) and a state-of-the-art Gymnasium, became operational in 2025.

Recreational/Extra Curricular activities


1. Sports: If you love sports, the Inter-IIT tournament is an event to cherish for a lifetime. IIT Jammu has been participating in the Inter-IIT sports meet since 2016. The teams have displayed great enthusiasm and sportspersonship, earning laurels in the respective sport. In addition to the Inter-IIT tournament, multiple Intra-IIT sports events, such as Pravaah, Convoquer, and Prarambh, are organized, providing ample opportunities for students to showcase their athletic talents and foster a competitive spirit within the institute. 

2. Clubs: IIT Jammu students participate regularly in Hackathons, Industry Conclaves, competitive coding, ACM ICPC and Google Summer Of Code (GSoC), etc. There are active student clubs for trekking, coding, arts, dramatics, literary expressions, robotics, SAE-Baja, and a social welfare group, Kritash. Outreach activities are frequently conducted by several student volunteers.  

3. Wellness: A gym costing 1 crore has been created for students, enhancing the wellness infrastructure at IIT Jammu. The institute hosts various events throughout the year focusing on yoga, meditation, and overall mind and body wellness. Further augmenting these facilities, an Indoor Sports Complex has been built, offering a comprehensive array of sports and wellness activities. This complex includes a new gym equipped with the latest equipment, a dedicated yoga and meditation hall, a table tennis hall, a multipurpose indoor hall with four badminton courts, a recreational hall, and two squash courts. These facilities are designed to promote physical fitness, mental well-being, and recreational activities among students. 

4. Student Council: Students manage several initiatives through elected members of the Student Council. Students show their creative side during the cultural fest Anhad. The institute provides ample opportunities to build event organization, interpersonal skills, team management, and leadership skills. Prof Sudhir Jain Leadership Award was created in 2020 to recognize such all-around contributions. 

Location and Accessibility


How to reach IIT Jammu, Jagti campus? 

Jammu is well connected by Air, Rail, and Road. 
- By Air: Jammu Airport at Satwari, around 15 km away.
- By Train: Jammu is a railway hub connected to many cities by train. It is about 13 km away.
- By Bus: Jammu bus stand is about 12 km away. 
- For local transport, there are matadors and J&K transport buses. 
- The buses available from the Railway Station and Bus Station for Katra drop you in front of the IIT Jammu Jagti campus.
