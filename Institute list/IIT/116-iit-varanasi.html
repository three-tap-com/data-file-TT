About the Institute

Engineering education at Banaras Hindu University (BHU) Varanasi was initiated in 1919 by the great visionary and patriot, Bharat Ratna Mahamana Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya Ji with the founding of Banaras Engineering College (BENCO). The next stage of development was the establishment of the College of Technology (TECHNO) and the College of Mining & Metallurgy (MINMET). In 1968, the erstwhile engineering colleges of BHU, namely BENCO, MINMET, and TECHNO, were merged to form the Institute of Technology (IT-BHU). On 29th June 2012, IT-BHU became the Indian Institute of Technology (Banaras Hindu University), Varanasi by an Act of Parliament. The Institute, since its inception, has maintained a high academic standard and produced distinguished engineers and administrators. The institute completed its 100 years in 2019 and is committed to taking forward the legacy with rejuvenated vigor and dedication to nation-building. As a mark of recognition of the Centenary Celebrations of the institute, Hon’ble Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi inaugurated the PARAM SHIVAY Supercomputer at IIT (BHU) Varanasi. He also released a postage stamp and postal album, which bore a picture of the main building of IIT(BHU). 

IIT (BHU) Varanasi is making significant reforms in the educational ecosystem and is dedicated to implementing the National Education Policy (NEP-2020), a novel vision bringing about a paradigm change in Indian education. Research and development are essential functions of the Indian Institute of Technology (BHU) Varanasi. The Institute promotes multidisciplinary academic programs and research for the country's knowledge-based economy. The institute collaborates with various academic and research institutions of high repute in India and abroad. IIT (BHU), Varanasi, has a global rank of 531 in the year 2025 QS (Quacquarelli Symonds) World University Rankings. Additionally, IIT(BHU) was ranked tenth in the NIRF 2024 Engineering category. 

The Institute has 15 departments and 3 interdisciplinary Schools. Central facilities in the Institute include the Supercomputing Centre, Computing and Information Services (CCIS), Central Instrumentation Facility (CIF), Main Workshop, and the Institute Shrinivas Deshpande Library. Besides this, the Center for Faculty Development and Educational Technology extends expert training in pedagogy, course delivery, and assessment.

Academic Structure

The Indian Institute of Technology (BHU) offers the 4-year B.Tech., 5-year B.Arch., 2-year M.Tech./M.Pharm./M.Sc., and Ph.D. degree programs. The institute has ten Engineering Departments (Ceramic Engineering, Chemical Engineering and Technology, Civil Engineering, Computer Science and Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Electronics Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Metallurgical Engineering, Mining Engineering and Pharmaceutical Engineering & Technology), three Science Departments (Chemistry, Mathematical Sciences and Physics) and three Interdisciplinary Schools (Biochemical Engineering, Biomedical Engineering and Materials Science and Technology). In addition, the Department of Humanistic Studies was established in the year 2015, while the Department of Architecture, Planning, and Design started its academic activities in 2019-20.

Research and Innovation are integral to the academic activities of the Institute. The academic curriculum encourages undergraduate students to take up research projects and industrial internships. The research and development activities are supported by different National Agencies, viz. Ministry of Human Resource and Development (MHRD), Department of Science and Technology (DST), Department of Biotechnology (DBT), All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR) and Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) through various programs. The following layout describes the academic opportunities available in the B.Tech. and B. Arch. course structures.  

A B.Tech. student has four mutually exclusive options at the end of the 4th semester. The option that a student gets depends on choice and merit. The following are these options.

a) The student can opt for a plain B.Tech. degree. The student doing plain B.Tech. may take up a Stream to get a B.Tech. Honours degree.

b)  A student can do a B.Tech. with a Minor in another discipline (other than the primary discipline). The primary discipline is one in which the student gets admission. A Minor consists of 4 to 6 courses in the chosen discipline (other than the primary discipline of the student) taught 5th semester onwards.  

c) Another option is a B.Tech. in the primary discipline with a second major in another branch. For a second major the tenure of the B.Tech. extends to 10 semesters. For example, a B.Tech. in Electrical Engineering with a second major in Computer Science and Engineering.

d) Students with a Minor in a particular discipline can change to a second Major in the same discipline at a later stage subject to seat vacancies and merit. For instance, it is possible for a student who is doing a B.Tech. in Metallurgical Engineering (the primary discipline) with a Minor in Mechanical Engineering to change to a second major in Mechanical Engineering, if there are vacancies. 

e) A B.Tech. student can opt for an integrated B.Tech & M.Tech Dual Degree in the primary discipline in 5 years (or 10 semesters). The integrated dual degree (IDD) aims to encourage and promote students to go for advanced studies and research in the primary discipline.

Furthermore, the 6th semester of B.Tech. curriculum in all branches is reserved for Industrial/research internships. 

B. Arch. is a 5-year degree. However, B.Arch. students also have access to the Minor and second major options mentioned above (for 4-year B.Tech. students). If a B.Arch. student wants to do a second major; then the degree term becomes 6 years. 

Credit System

IIT(BHU) follows the semester system. An academic year (July-May) consists of two semesters each of approximately 18 weeks duration. Each theory and laboratory course has a certain number of credits assigned to it depending on the lecture, tutorial, and laboratory contact hours a week. Each course is coordinated by a Coordination Committee, which has full responsibility for coordinating the course, holding the periodical and end-semester examinations, and awarding letter grades on a 10-point scale on a relative grading basis. A student's performance is measured by the number of credits earned and the Semester Performance Index (SPI)/Cumulative Performance Index (CPI) earned for a semester/course.

Facilities

The institute has well-equipped laboratories and workshops, excellent computer facilities in all Departments/Schools in addition to a Central Computer Facility. The other facilities are:

Supercomputing Centre

A Supercomputing Center has been set up in the Institute under the National Supercomputing Mission. The supercomputer PARAM Shivay has a peak processing speed of 833 TeraFLOPS. Sixty percent of the processing power is for local use by the IIT(BHU) and BHU research community, and the rest for other CFTIs and research labs across the nation. The system has been commissioned under the Make in India program, and the hardware has been manufactured at a plant in Chennai by the French company Atos. The system is a sophisticated mix of CPUs and GPUs with relevant systems and application software based on open source. Inaugurating the IIT (BHU) Supercomputing Center on 19th February 2019, the Honorable Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi said, "...the land of Lord Vishwanath will now contribute in accelerating research in key areas across the Nation by providing fast computation facility through the PARAM Shivay system...”. The Institute has organized a series of hands-on workshops to impart expertise to the user community for the utilization of the facility. 

Library Facilities

The library system at the Indian Institute of Technology (BHU), Varanasi, comprises the Shreeniva Deshpande Library in addition to five departmental libraries, which collectively facilitate the educational, research, and outreach initiatives of the institution. In addition to possessing an extensive physical collection exceeding 150,000 volumes of books, journals, theses, reports, and pamphlets, the library system also grants access to over 15,000 electronic journals and more than 7,000 electronic books, e-standards, and databases pertinent to the fields of science, engineering, and technology. The library offers a variety of facilities, including reading rooms, access to a Digital Library, a web-based Online Public Access Catalogue (OPAC), remote access to electronic resources, a discussion room, and reference services that are integral to research and teaching endeavors. Furthermore, the library has established the Indian Research Information Network System (IRINS), which serves as a database of the profiles of the Institute's researchers and includes an Institutional Repository. The library further enhances research activities by offering a suite of Research Support Tools, which encompasses Anti-Plagiarism software, Grammarly, EndNote, CSD-Materials, Overleaf, ChemDraw, and various reference management tools. The implementation of RFID for library services is going on. 

Hostel Facilities

The Institute has fifteen hostels for boys and three for girls. All the hostels are equipped with modern amenities. The messes are managed very well by the students and wardens. There is an Institute Cafeteria, which runs during Institute working hours.

Medical Facilities

The health problems of the students are looked after by the Students Health Care Complex of the University. For specialized treatments, the consultation of senior specialists is available from the renowned Sir Sunder Lal Hospital attached to the Institute of Medical Sciences of the Banaras Hindu University.

The Institute subscribes to a Group Medical Insurance scheme for students from the 2019-20 session. A fee is charged every Odd Semester from all registered students. 

Student Life at Institute

The University campus is designed as a Semi-Circle with different wings located in different sectors. Thus, the IIT(BHU) campus has all the Hostels located in the outer sector, and academic wings are in the inner sector with playgrounds in between. There are many recreational facilities for the all-round personality development of the students. Besides these, the students can also make the best use of the facilities, such as certificate courses in foreign languages, music, fine arts, etc., of the Banaras Hindu University.

Financial Assistance

Many scholarships are available to undergraduate students. A good number of Endowment Scholarships are also available to students in specified disciplines. Some of these scholarships are on a loan basis. The Indian Institute of Technology (BHU) is also one of the recognized institutions for the award of scholarships to SC/ST students under the Central Sector Scholarship Scheme of the top-class education scheme of the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment and the Ministry of Tribal Welfare Offices. IDD students are provided with an Institute Fellowship at the Part-V level if they fulfill certain basic criteria. All types of Scholarships/Fellowships are announced on the Institute website.

Training & Placement

Training and Placement Cell of the Institute is actively involved in arranging the in-campus internship and placement of students in various industries and organizations.  and arranges summer practical training which is an essential component of academic curricula. This cell also organizes soft skill development programs and gives pre-placement training in various aspects. The placement record of the Institute has been excellent, with job offers mostly from organizations like Microsoft, Hindustan Unilever, De Shaw, NTPC, Deloitte, Morgan Stanley, BHEL, GAIL, Goldman Sachs, Indian Oil, Bharat Petroleum, DRDO, Tata Motors, etc.

Large numbers of students are also opting for higher studies in reputed universities all over the world. The industry-oriented internship program is aimed at inculcating professional culture amongst the students and familiarizing them with the work environment in the industry. The students are also encouraged to opt for internships and research projects in industrial/ R&D/reputed educational institutions in foreign countries.

Industry And Alumni Relations

The alumni of the Institute are in apex positions of esteemed organizations/institutions in India and abroad. The Institute is proud of its alumni community, constantly volunteering for support and guidance. The office of the Dean (Resource and Alumni) organizes frequent engaging sessions and formal/informal meetings to strengthen the bond between the alumni and the students. The Alumni of the Institute fund several major endowment scholarships, medals, awards, and other donations.

Recreational/Extra Curricular activities

The analytical, creative, and managerial skills of the students get a chance to be honed through activities of the vibrant Institute Gymkhana, which has cultural, sports & games, and co-curricular wings.

The Institute has full-fledged cricket and football grounds, as well as volleyball, basketball, and hard-court tennis courts. The Students Activity Center (SAC) of the institute houses state-of-the-art gymnasiums, badminton courts, table tennis, yoga, and similar activity spaces. All the hostels have facilities for indoor games and entertainment. Gymkhana activities are mainly managed by the students to promote artistic and creative talents in dramatics, elocution, music, and visual arts. Gymkhana has various active clubs, viz. HAM, Audio, Photography, Automobile and Aeromodelling, Cine, Computer, Astronomy, etc. Students actively take part in managing three national-level events every year, viz. All India Cultural Festival ''KASHI-YATRA'', all India Sports Competition ''SPARDHA'', and All India Exhibition of Engineering and Technology Models ''TECHNEX''.

Design and Innovation Centre (DIC)

DIC is a joint project of IIT-BHU and BHU. It has been established to work on the possibilities of innovation in Technology and Humanities, Liberal Arts, Social Science, Art, Culture, Music, languages, and other relevant areas. DIC, IIT (BHU) and DIC, BHU works as HUB, and the center has three spokes; (i) Indian Institute of Information Technology, Allahabad, (ii) Motilal Nehru National Institute of Technology, Allahabad, (iii) University of Allahabad.

The Center has also established three labs, namely Graphic and Digital Media Lab, Digital Innovation Gallery & Design Cafe (Prototype Lab and Workshop Place). Graphic and Digital Media Lab provides the facility for graphic design, web design, mobile app design, imaging, animation, documentaries, design counseling, etc. The Digital Innovation Gallery works as an exhibition platform for IIT-BHU, BHU, and other spokes of DIC-BHU. A modern prototype lab has also been established for students.
